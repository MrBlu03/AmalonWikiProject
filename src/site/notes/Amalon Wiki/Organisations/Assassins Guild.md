# Overview

The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] in [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Aradoth\|Amalon]] is a significant organization, known for its members' skills in combat and subterfuge. The guild is not only a place for trained killers, but also a place that provides a unique service. The guild is known for offering its services to the highest bidder, and sometimes they take on jobs for less than reputable purposes.

### Structure

The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] appears to have a clear leadership structure and a variety of roles within its organization.

### Leadership

- [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]] is a prominent figure, described as the second son of [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Minor Characters/Haki Bin\|Haki Bin]]. He is the leader of the [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]]. He is also known as "Sir Eagle" by his assassins.
- [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]] is a capable leader who demands obedience and respect from his assassins.
- There is mention of a weapons keeper at the [[Amalon Wiki/Notable Locations/The Drunken Sails Tavern\|The Drunken Sails Tavern]], although this person doesn't appear to be a leader of the guild.

### Ranks

- The sources do not provide a detailed list of specific ranks. However, it can be inferred that the guild has members with varying skills and responsibilities.
- Some assassins are noted for their expertise in combat, such as Aya, who wields a rapier called Deathwisp and has unmatched speed and deadly strikes.
- Gy'Zin is referred to as [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]] 's "trusty guard", suggesting he is a high ranking member of the guild.
- Other members include Emara and Aerithum, who are described as assassins within the guild. It can also be inferred that the guild has scouts.
- It appears that distinguished fighters within the guild have opportunities to specialize in new weapons.

### Locations

- The guild has a hidden headquarters located in the basement of an abandoned building. This headquarters has gold-covered walls and animal heads displayed.
- The guild is known to operate in the city of [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Gilded Juval]], where they offer their services to the highest bidder.
- The guild also has connections to [[Magic and Technology/Technology/Clockwork Technology\|Clockwork Technology]], which they use for more volatile and dangerous purposes.

### Activities

- The guild engages in various types of assassinations.
- The guild accepts contracts from various clients, sometimes for less than reputable purposes.
- They engage in smuggling operations.
- They gather information.
- The guild uses a variety of weapons such as:
    - Rapiers, used by Aya
    - Swords, used by many members of the guild
    - Bows, used by [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]]
    - Daggers, also used by [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]]

### Relationships with other Factions

- The guild has a history of working with the [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Thymr\|Vileblood]].
- It is known that the guild has an alliance with the [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Whisperers\|Darkweavers]].

### Additional Notes

- The guild is known for being discreet and secretive.
- The guild has a rule against contracts outside of [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Juval]].
- The guild is willing to assist people in escaping danger if the price is right.
- Some assassins within the guild have unusual abilities, such as the ability to control shadows.

## Methods and Practices

### Training

- The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] trains its members in various combat styles, including swordsmanship.
- Members are taught to respect honour, but to prioritize survival, as "no one plays fair when saving their skin".
- Training emphasizes the use of momentum and structure, not just brute strength, in attacks.
- Distinguished fighters within the guild get the chance to specialise in a new weapon, often taking years to earn this kind of promotion.
- Some members, like [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]], train in both close-range and long-range weaponry.
- Assassins also train in agility, such as running and jumping over obstacles.
- Members of the guild also learn how to use tools such as throwing knives and bows.
- Training is rigorous, with an emphasis on speed, precision, and adaptability.
- Some members of the guild are also trained as scouts.
- [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Mar'Keth Vithyaini\|Mar'Keth]] is known to be a mentor to [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]].

### Rules and Code

- The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] operates with a degree of secrecy and discretion.
- Assassins are expected to follow orders and fulfill their contracts.
- Members of the guild are expected to be loyal to the guild and its leader.
- The guild has a rule against taking contracts outside of [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Juval]].

### Tools and Techniques

- The guild utilizes a variety of weapons, including:
    - Rapiers, such as the "Deathwisp" wielded by Aya, known for its unmatched speed and deadly strikes.
    - Swords, used by many members.
    - Bows and arrows, used by [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]].
    - Daggers, concealed and used by [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]].
    - [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Thymr\|Vileblood]] weapons; [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]] is able to create weapons out of [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Thymr\|Vileblood]].
- The guild also uses poison on their blades.
- The guild uses [[Magic and Technology/Technology/Clockwork Technology\|Clockwork Technology]], and they reverse engineer stolen blueprints to create volatile and dangerous products, such as explosives and advanced communication devices like the "[[Magic and Technology/Technology/Aegis Technology\|Aegis Eye]]".
- Assassins within the guild are skilled in both stealth and direct combat.
- The guild is known to use motion bombs, that detonate using pressure on a fuse.
- Some members may use specialized weapons such as a flail.

## Known Operations

- The guild accepts contracts for assassinations and other tasks, sometimes from less reputable clients in [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Gilded Juval]].
- They engage in smuggling operations.
- The guild gathers information through various means.
- The guild is willing to help people escape danger if the price is right.
- The guild is known to have been involved in protecting convoys.
- The guild has been hired to assassinate political figures.
- They work with the [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Whisperers\|Darkweavers]] and the [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/La-Krosh\|La-Krosh]] to use their powers.

## Relationships

### Allies

- The guild has a past alliance with [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Thymr\|Vileblood]].
- The guild works with the [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Whisperers\|Darkweavers]].

### Rivals

- The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] has a history of conflict with the Jat'Mey Guild.
- The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] is known to have worked with individuals who are also in conflict with the [[Amalon Wiki/Organisations/Silver Army\|Silver Army]].
- The guild is in conflict with [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/High Valomor\|Valomorian]] groups.
- There is conflict between the guild and [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Macijek A'Vak\|Macijek A'Vak]].

## Cultural Impact

The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] has a significant influence on society in [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Gilded Juval]] and beyond, operating as a powerful and feared organisation.
- The guild's existence contributes to the culture of political intrigue and danger within [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Gilded Juval]].
- The guild's willingness to take on contracts for a variety of clients, including less reputable ones, indicates a flexible moral code, suggesting a society where money and power can often override ethical considerations.
- The guild is known for its discretion and secrecy, which adds to its mystique and fear factor.
- The guild's actions can impact the political and economic landscape of [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Aradoth\|Amalon]], as they are involved in assassinations, smuggling and information gathering.
- The people of [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Gilded Juval]] are aware of the guild, and some seek to join it for purposes of revenge.
- The guild's reputation for ruthlessness and efficiency makes it a subject of both fear and respect.

### Notable Members

- [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]], also known as "Sir Eagle," is the current leader of the guild. He is a skilled fighter, trained in both melee and ranged combat, with the ability to control [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Thymr\|Vileblood]]. He is the second son of [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Minor Characters/Haki Bin\|Haki Bin]].
- Aya is a highly skilled assassin known for his unmatched speed and deadly strikes with his rapier, "Deathwisp". He is a former merchant from [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Ervenia\|Ervenia]] with a thirst for vengeance.
- Gy'Zin is [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]] 's "trusty guard" and is likely a high-ranking member of the guild.
- [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Mar'Keth Vithyaini\|Mar'Keth]], while not a member of the guild, is a mentor to [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]], and has connections to the guild, and is also known to have trained with them. He was known as the "Monstrous Puncher" and was known for winning punch-offs. He was also known to be a skilled fighter, and is an important figure from [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/R'oth Vilgmeri\|Vilgmeri]].
- Emara is an assassin within the guild.
- Aerithum is another assassin within the guild, and is known to have some level of responsibility.
- It is known that the guild has a weapons keeper that works at the [[Amalon Wiki/Notable Locations/The Drunken Sails Tavern\|The Drunken Sails Tavern]], although their name is not mentioned.
- The guild employs scouts that work alongside the assassins.

### Behind the Scenes

- The [[Assassin's Guild\|Assassin's Guild]] is a significant power player in [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Aradoth\|Amalon]], with influence over both political and economic affairs.
- The guild is capable of carrying out complex operations that can change the course of events in [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Aradoth\|Amalon]].
- The guild's activities, such as assassinations, smuggling and information gathering, often have far-reaching consequences.
- The guild's use of [[Magic and Technology/Technology/Clockwork Technology\|Clockwork Technology]] and alliances with groups like the [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Whisperers\|Darkweavers]] and [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/La-Krosh\|La-Krosh]] suggest a willingness to embrace dangerous and unconventional methods.
- The guild serves as a reflection of the moral ambiguity and political instability that exists in [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Aradoth\|Amalon]], particularly in [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/Gilded Juval\|Gilded Juval]].
- The guild was involved in a conflict against the [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/High Valomor\|Valomorians]], during which they lost a lot of people.
- The guild was also involved in a conflict with [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Macijek A'Vak\|Macijek A'Vak]].
- [[Amalon Wiki/Characters/Major Characters/Ryo Bin\|Ryo Bin]] was hired by a [[Amalon Wiki/The Kingdoms/High Valomor\|Valomorian]] magistrate to kill a student who had bonded with the astral, as well as to kill members of the council.
- It is implied that some of the guild are able to work with [[Magic and Technology/Mythological Beings & Entities/The Thymr\|Vileblood]].
- The guild has a network of contacts, including the [[Amalon Wiki/Notable Locations/The Drunken Sails Tavern\|The Drunken Sails Tavern]], which serves as a safe place for the guild.
